Conjectures and Problems

Below are some open questions related to my work, which might be interesting to others as well to look at. Needless to say, I would be glad to learn about any progress or solutions.

Rationality of function fields

Let k be a field of characteristic 0, t a transcendental, and E/k(t), F/k(t) extensions in a finite Galois extension L/k(t). Suppose that E and F are arithmetically equivalent, that is, the two permutation characters of the Galois group of L/k(t) corresponding to E and F are the same. Does rationality of E imply rationality of F?
I believe the answer is no, but it does not seem to be easy to produce examples.

An extension of Burnside/Schur/Wielandt on primitive permutation groups

Let G be a faithful finite primitive permutation group, and C a cyclic subgroup. If C is transitive, then classical results by Burnside and Schur imply that G is doubly transitive (or solvable of prime degree).
In applications to Hilbert's irreducibility theorem we need to know something about G if we require that C has two orbits. As a consequence of a horrible classification we obtain that G has permutation rank at most 3, which means, that a point stabilizer has at most 3 orbits. The proof uses the classification of the finite simple groups.
I would like to see a direct and classification-free proof, maybe in the spirit of Wielandt's where this is shown if the degree of G is twice a prime number.
