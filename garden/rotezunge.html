Rote Zunge

In 1992 I obtained a capsule from a cut style pollination of a white Lilium regale seedling with Woodriff's Bloodtiger. The capsule became soft quite early. At that early stage it would have been impossible to remove the immature embryos from the seeds. Not hoping for much, I sliced the capsule in a couple of pieces and put the slices in vitro. From the resulting callus one seedling developed.
Because of a two-year stay in Florida from 1995 to 1997, we potted the little bulb. It survived with little care on the balcony at my parents in law in Hungary.
It bloomed for the first time in 1996. In 1997, right after our return from Florida, it bloomed again. Lizi (our older daughter) named it Rote Zunge, which is German for `red tongue'. At that time, the lower petal of each flower was straight and not reflexed -- it looked if someone would stick out his tongue. This kind of flower type hardly occured later, in fact the flowers are quite symmetric. Apparenty, this was caused by the extreme culture conditions -- potted and placed on a hot balcony in southern Hungary.
I made a chromosome count, Rote Zunge is diploid. Presently I'm trying to convert it to tetraploidy by a colchicine treatment of scales. So far Rote Zunge has been sterile, though it occasionally produced firm capsules with fertile tetraploids. Also pollen development is abnormal. Most of the time there is no pollen at all, while in some years the anthers are fully developed.
