Applied Algebra and Discrete Mathematics

Organizers:Gábor P. Nagy (Szeged), Miklós Maróti (Szeged), Peter Müller (Würzburg)
  • In the autumn semester 2014 we organize an intensive course in Applied Algebra and Discrete Mathematics.
  • The aim is to present some relevant problems in this field, to understand the theoretical background and to implement and compare the computational tools which can deal with these problems.
  • The intensive course consists of two parts: a regular course Applied Discrete Mathematics (MV9990) at the University of Szeged and 2-weeks Winter School at the University of Würzburg.
  • We plan to present the problems and the theoretical background in the regular course and focus on the implementations in the Winter School.
  • The Winter School is open for everybody, the preparation can be made individually.

Curriculum

Coding Theory

  • Basic concepts of Information Theory and Error Correcting Codes
    Noisy channel, Shannon's Theorem, block codes, linear codes.
    Length, dimension, Hamming distance, weight, weight distribution, minimum distance,
    Generating matrix, parity check matrix, syndrom decoding.
  • Weight distribution of random linear codes
    Vladimir Blinovsky, Uri Erez, Simon Litsyn
    Weight distribution moments of random linear/coset codes
    Tests for random binary and q-ary codes
  • Reed-Solomon codes and Berlekamp-Massey decoding
    S. Gao
    A NEW ALGORITHM FOR DECODING REED-SOLOMON CODES
    Implementation in GUAVA
  • LDPC codes and iterative belief propagation decoding
  • Bit error rate, package error rate, BER curve

Complexity Theory

  • Basic concepts of Complexity Theory
  • Graphs and algorithms
    Automorphisms, isomorphisms, invariants
  • NP-complete problems and their solvers
    3-SAT ➙ Wikipedia
    Clique ➙ CLIQUER
    Exact Cover ➙ Dancing Links (DLX)
  • Reformulating problems

Relaxations of combinatorial optimization problems

  • Linear Programming relaxations
  • Semidefinite Programming and combinatorial optimization
    Goemans-Williamson MAX CUT algorithm
    Lovasz Theta function of small graphs
  • Integer Linear Programming, LLL algorithm
  • Groebner basis methods
